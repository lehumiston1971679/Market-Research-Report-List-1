<p><p>The Trivalent Chromium Finishing market includes three main types: Plating, Conversion Coatings, and Passivation. Plating involves depositing a thin layer of trivalent chromium onto surfaces for corrosion resistance, durability, and aesthetic appeal. Conversion Coatings chemically transform the metal surface into a protective layer, enhancing adhesion and corrosion resistance. Passivation creates a passive oxide layer on metals like stainless steel, improving corrosion resistance and surface stability. All three methods are eco-friendly alternatives to hexavalent chromium, widely used in automotive, aerospace, and electronics industries.</p></p>
