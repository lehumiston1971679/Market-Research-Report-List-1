<p><ul><li>Impregnation method</li><li>CVD method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Carbon matrix composites are advanced materials featuring a carbon matrix reinforced with various fibers. The market includes two primary types based on manufacturing methods. The impregnation method involves saturating a fibrous substrate with a carbon-rich liquid or polymer, which is then carbonized to create a dense composite. In contrast, the Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) method involves depositing carbon atoms from a gas phase onto a substrate, forming a strong and uniform composite. Both methods are crucial for diverse applications, including aerospace and energy sectors.</p></p>
