<p><p>The tissue-engineered bone market encompasses three primary types: inorganic materials, organic materials, and composite materials. Inorganic materials, such as calcium phosphate and bioactive glass, provide structural support and mimic natural bone properties. Organic materials, typically derived from collagen or chitosan, enhance cell attachment and proliferation. Composite materials combine inorganic and organic components, leveraging the strengths of both to improve biocompatibility and mechanical performance. This diversification supports a wide range of applications in bone regeneration and repair.</p></p>
